Living in Brandon, Florida — What to Know Before You Move

By William Luke Holland · June 2026 · ← Back to Blog

Why Brandon?

Brandon is one of Tampa Bay's most established and sought-after suburban communities — sitting about 12 miles east of downtown Tampa in Hillsborough County. It offers a blend of affordable housing, good schools, convenient access to major highways, and a genuine community feel that's increasingly rare in Florida's fast-growing metro areas.

If you're considering a move to the Tampa Bay area and haven't explored Brandon yet, here's what you need to know.

Location & Commute

Brandon's biggest practical advantage is its location. You're on I-75 and I-4, which means relatively easy access to Tampa, Lakeland, and even Orlando. The typical commute to downtown Tampa runs 20-35 minutes depending on traffic. Brandon also sits close to the Crosstown Expressway, which cuts straight into Tampa.

If you work in the eastern or southeastern Tampa area — near Sabal Park, the Brandon Mall corridor, or USF — Brandon may be your most logical choice.

Neighborhoods Within Brandon

Brandon isn't just one neighborhood — it's a collection of distinct communities worth exploring:

  • Bloomingdale: Established, tree-lined streets, excellent schools, strong community feel
  • FishHawk Ranch: Master-planned, resort-style amenities, newer construction, top-rated schools
  • Valrico: Slightly more rural feel, larger lots, growing rapidly
  • Brandon proper: More affordable entry points, convenient to shopping and dining

Schools

Brandon falls within the Hillsborough County School District, with several highly-rated elementary and middle schools. FishHawk Ranch in particular is known for some of the best public schools in the county. If schools are a priority, it's worth narrowing your search to specific attendance zones — and we're happy to help you do that.

The Real Estate Market in Brandon

Brandon's housing market remains one of the more accessible entry points in the Tampa Bay area. Single-family homes range from the mid-$200s for older, smaller properties to well over $500k for newer builds or larger homes in premium communities like FishHawk.

Inventory has improved over the past year, which means buyers have more negotiating room than they did in 2022-2023. Homes that are priced right and presented well still sell quickly — but gone are the days of waiving inspections and offering $50k over asking.

Is Brandon Right for You?

Brandon tends to be a great fit for families who want good schools, a real neighborhood feel, reasonable commutes, and more house for the money than you'd get closer to Tampa proper. It's also popular with first-time buyers who want to stay within reach of the city without paying downtown prices.
